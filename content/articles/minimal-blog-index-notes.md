# minimal blog index notes

This is a short companion article for reviewing the articles index with more
than one entry. It is intentionally quieter than the Vemi syntax page so the
list has different title lengths, tag counts, and descriptions.

## why it exists

One article can make almost any layout look acceptable. Two entries reveal
whether the spacing has enough structure, whether metadata is too loud, and
whether the page starts to feel like a real archive instead of a placeholder.

## what to look for

- the date should establish chronology
- read time should be helpful but secondary
- tags should add texture without becoming buttons
- descriptions should clarify the article without becoming summaries

> A minimal page still needs enough rhythm to feel intentional.

```txt
title
meta
description
```

That is the whole shape for now.
